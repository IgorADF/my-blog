Clifford was never meant to be the biggest dog on the block, but life had other plans. He grew, and grew, and then grew some more — until the mailbox looked like a toy and the neighbors started measuring him in doorframes.

What stayed small was his sense of mischief: a tail that cleared coffee tables, a bark that rattled windows, and an unshakable conviction that every lap was his lap.
